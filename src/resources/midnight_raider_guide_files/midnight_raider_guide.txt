What’s changed for Raiders in Midnight?

If you don’t care about any of this, you can skip to here or use the sheet or addon.


Blizzard has harkened back to the days of Mists and Cata where we are getting a tier split amongst multiple raids with a slightly staggered release date including a heroic week. Week 1 of Season 1 will have mythic 0 dungeons and include LFR, Normal, and Heroic difficulties of the 6 boss Voidspire raid and the 1 boss Dreamrift raid. Week 2 will include the mythic versions of these raids and unlock M+ as well. Week 3 will unlock the LFR, Normal, Heroic, and Mythic versions of the 2 boss raid March on Quel’danas, which will contain the Omni-Token and the two hardest bosses of the season. There are some advantages to this - split raid IDs opens the possibility for not having to extend earlier in the season if you still want to reclear. If you’re working on the 6th boss of Voidspire, you can still clear Dreamrift quickly. If you’re working on March of Quel’danas, you could quickly clear the first 3 bosses of Voidspire and the Dreamrift to get 2 raid vault slots each week while still working on the March bosses. For the truly degenerate amongst you, you could even try to do mythic Dreamrift splits because it won’t affect the lockout of Voidspire or March. I look forward to seeing what y’all cook up!


Next, the changes to gearing in Midnight.

Crests are now track-specific. Hero track items require heroic crests only, mythic track items require mythic crests only etc. That means you can upgrade hero items to 5/6 and 6/6 and not sabotage your mythic progression. In fact, you can enhance it!
They removed valorstones! YAY! They made it cost a miniscule amount of gold instead for some reason! Less YAY!
The champion track (normal raid difficulty) is reduced to a 6 rank track. Items at 6/6 champion are the equivalent of 2/6 hero, making normal mode trinkets, tier, etc. weaker than they currently are.
Upgrade costs have changed to 20 crests per upgrade, all the way from 1/6 to 6/6.
The crest cap is raised from 90 to 100. This gives you 5 upgrades per week instead of the previous 6 upgrades per week.
Crafting items now costs 80 Crests. This is the same amount that it takes to create an item at 5/6 so nothing has changed.

A small change with tier tokens. Tier tokens are now sorted by armor type. Tier tokens from The Dreamrift have the prefix “aln-” and Tier tokens from The Voidspire have the prefix “void-”. Plate wearers use the Alnforged and Voidforged tokens, Mail wearers use the Alncast and Voidcast tokens, Leather wearers uses the Alncured and Voidcured tokens, and Cloth wearers use the Alnwoven and Voidwoven tokens. Be aware that the Omnitoken will not become available until the 3rd week.


Another nice change is that once you acquire your 4-set bonus for the first time, all content can drop catalyst charges going forward. This makes queueing for LFR to try to get tier pieces so that you can activate your 4set bonus ASAP very important. Once unlocked, you can catalyze that higher item level non-set piece from M+ easily and still guarantee the ability to catalyze the random mythic armor piece you get from your vault.


There is a new system called Prey that raiders can engage in for some loot in the first couple weeks but just like Delves the loot will fall off hard once Mythic+ unlocks. It comes in three difficulties - Normal, Hard, and Nightmare - that respectively award weekly Adventurer/Veteran/Champion rewards and crests. Full information can be found here and here.


For Delve lovers, the way loot is awarded hasn’t changed much - just some modifications along the Delver’s Journey. I highly recommend keeping up with Moleman’s Delve Compendium if you are interested in Delves and their various rewards.


There are a wide variety of consumables following in the same track as Dragonflight and TWW. You have your typical primary, secondary, and tertiary enchants. The only new twists are a crit damage ring enchant and an embellishment that doubles the strength of the special crafted Jewelcrafting rings and amulets. There are the normal sharpening stones by Blacksmiths but there’s also a new enchanting stone that gives melee attacks an arcane damage proc. While these are interesting, we all know we’re just going to do what sims tell us to do :( Food buffs return as highest secondary and primary stat but also increase your stamina as well, providing some extra survivability for those early weeks. You can still combine 10 feasts or 5 food buffs to make them hearty.


There are a wide variety of new renowns that award Champion item level pieces at a renown level very easily reached after the campaign, especially with the DMF 10% buff for side quests. The Renown reward items are head, waist, necklace, and trinket. These provide a small but tangible power boost leading into the first Heroic raid. There is no raid renown in Midnight Season 1. What are the ramifications of the new upgrade system?


Normal Difficulty(champion) items are now much less useful and powerful! Because Champion track items cap out at the equivalent of a 2/6 Heroic item instead of a 4/6 Heroic item, your Champion trinkets and set pieces will no longer have the staying power they did previously. You’ll want to replace them as quickly as possible.


Hero Crests are now much more useful and powerful! This is a great change, giving players the ability to continue improving their Heroic items for about 6 weeks. This is a massive power increase for Heroic items and will increase your average item level by a surprising amount earlier in the season. In addition, there are some interesting ways to save Myth crests now.


Crafted items are only Crest efficient if compared to a 1/6 item. If you are a player that consistently gets 2/6 mythic items from drops or vault, you will not want to spend Myth crests on these items other than to get your Embellishments or if you have a surplus of Crests and want a temporary power boost.


Early mythic progression vault breakpoints continue to be very powerful. Every item in your vault you claim at 3/6 or 4/6 is going to be an ilvl increase over other people. Unless they change M+ vault to having scaling ilvl drops, taking an item from your m+ track is going to feel bad compared to taking it from your raid vault. Taking a 3/6 from your vault is the equivalent of getting 40% more Crests per week - taking a 4/6 is 60% more Crests per week.




New - What changed with the Feb 20 Crest Changes?

For those who are looking at this for the first time since the change from scaling Crest costs, Blizzard has reverted that. Click the link above for full details. Below are the changes to the guide that were made after this was changed.


1) Weapons from raid and vault are competitive again.

2) Crafting is once again only good for your first two items if you are a raider.You probably won’t craft beyond this if you have other items to upgrade.

3) You now only hold Crests for 1 week, using them before stepping into Mythic.

4) Your item level per week is much higher. For example, by the third week of mythic you should be 2.67 average ilvls higher than you would’ve been under the previous system.

5) You will be able to convert Hero crests to Myth crests through some interesting shenanigans.



How do I properly upgrade my gear?


Spend heroic crests after you are done with reclear on Week 2, before stepping into Mythic for the first time. Unless your team cannot clear Heroic without spending these upgrades, it’s a good way to make sure that you get some crest saves from M+ and the later bosses in heroic that can drop higher item level.


When upgrading Mythic pieces, if it’s a 1/6 mythic item, you have the ability to convert Heroic crests into Mythic crests. For example, if you get a 1/6 mythic helmet from your vault, you can upgrade your hero track item in that slot to 5/6 which is the same item level as 1/6 mythic for free. At this point, you can upgrade your 5/6 heroic helmet to 6/6 which then lets you upgrade your 1/6 mythic helmet to 2/6 for free, effectively converting 20 Heroic crests to 20 Mythic crests. Because you’ll be done with Heroic crests around week 6, any extra crests you can convert to mythic are helpful. Proof this works, courtesy of Zorthas. Thanks Zorthas!



New - Crafting Advice

There are two possible crafting paths for classes that can wield 2-handed weapons. Dual wield classes almost certainly will craft a 1h (fury warriors 2h) but for the classes that can use a 2-handed weapon you have two options each with their own benefits and risks. Note: int users, the only 2handed weapon from the raid comes from the final boss which you cannot obtain until after progression ends. This may weight in to your decision.


Path 1 - Crafting a mythic 2h weapon ASAP

Benefits: you gain a 5/6 (285) equivalent mythic weapon ASAP for the massive power spike for 80 Myth Crests.

Negatives: You only have 1 embellishment, you spend 2 sparks to do so, and your weapon is 4 ilvls below max. You probably have unused Mythic crests for a couple weeks (see guide for timeline).


Path 2 - Upgrading a 3/6H 2h Heroic weapon from M+ to 6/6h and crafting in 2 other spots

Benefits: You gain 2x 5/6 (285) equivalent mythic armor slots with 2 embellishments for 160 Myth crests. When you do get a Mythic weapon, you can upgrade it to 6/6M for 80 Myth crests and max ilvl (289). You get to use your Mythic crests earlier for more power gain.

Negatives: You use a 6/6h(2/6m equivalent) weapon until you get a mythic weapon from vault or raid drop, which will lower your power. There’s no guarantee you ever get a Mythic weapon from vault or raid. However, even if you get just a one-handed weapon, you could still use a 6/6m main hand and a 6/6h(same ilvl as 2/6m) offhand.


Each class Discord will recommend what they think is best. If you’re a raider, and your raid leader says they need the immediate power boost of a 2h weapon, please listen to them so I don’t get DMs from angry raid leaders about having a raid team full of people sandbagging their weapon ilvl. However, depending on the tuning, having 2x items at myth item level with 2 embellishments might be more powerful than having 9 ilvls on your weapon.
